ANNUAL REPORT 2024 - OPERATIONAL UPDATE

PRODUCTION PERFORMANCE

Reported Production 2024: 888 mboe/d (million barrels of oil equivalent per day)
- 4.4% lower than 2023
- Underlying production: down 2.8% for the full year
- Major decline mainly due to Egypt production
- Partially offset by major projects ramp-up

RENEWABLES PIPELINE

End of Year Renewables Pipeline: 60.6 GW (Gigawatt)
- LSGp (Low Specific Gross Pipeline): 38.7 GW
- Net increase of 2.3 GW during the year
- Result of significant LSGp reductions due to high-grading
- Focus on proposed hydrogen projects
- Major projects ramp-up

PRODUCTION FACTORS

Key Production Drivers:
1. Egypt production decline - significant impact
2. Major projects ramp-up - offset decline
3. Underlying production down 2.8% annually
4. Focus on renewable energy expansion

CAPITAL AND INVESTMENTS

Major Projects Status:
- In ramp-up phase
- Expected to offset production declines
- Strategic focus on hydrogen projects
- Renewable energy infrastructure development

STRATEGIC INITIATIVES

Key Focus Areas:
1. High-grading of operations
2. Proposed hydrogen projects
3. Renewable energy pipeline expansion
4. Production efficiency improvements

BUSINESS SEGMENTS

Production:
- Total: 888 mboe/d (2024)
- Previous year: 928 mboe/d (2023)
- Change: 4.4% decrease

Renewables:
- Pipeline capacity: 60.6 GW
- LSGp in portfolio: 38.7 GW
- Growth trajectory: +2.3 GW year-on-year

SUMMARY

The company reported production of 888 mboe/d for 2024, representing a 4.4% decrease from 2023.
The underlying production decline of 2.8% was mainly driven by Egypt production challenges,
but was partially offset by major projects ramping up. The renewables pipeline grew to 60.6 GW
with 38.7 GW in the LSGp, showing a net increase of 2.3 GW during the year through strategic
high-grading and focus on hydrogen projects.
